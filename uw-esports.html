Washington eSports

Context

In 2014, University of Washington won the League of Legends North American Collegiate Championship, winning $52,000 in scholarships. The next year, UW failed to even qualify. This decline was caused primarily by the lack of infrastructure at UW to produce a top-tier eSports team and last minute player conflicts and negotiations between multiple teams wishing to represent the University of Washington. With these events fresh in mind I set out to find a way to prevent conflicts of a similiar nature and thus Washington eSports was created. Check out some of the awesome opportunities I was able to engage in and materials I helped create.

Highlights

  • Founded Washington eSports and managed the League of Legends team.
  • League of Legends team placed top 8 in "uLol Series".
  • Heroes of Storm team placed top 8 in "Heroes of the Dorm."
  • Over $10,000 in scholorship money won
  • Documentation featured at Riot Games Collegiate Summit

Objective Identification

In order to create Washington eSpotrs, the first task was to figure out the objectives it is trying to achieve. After meeting, interviewing, and brainstorming with various key stakeholders; the following objectives were identified:

  • Establish a team that will give University of Washington the highest chance to win the collegiate league.
  • Provide support and resources, to players so that they can succeed and meet both personal and team goals.
  • Develop players and personnel to ensure the long-term success of University of Washington in collegiate eSports.
  • Represent the University of Washington and its students in a manner that reflects the values of our school and demonstrates professionalism.
  • Lobby relevant parties for recognition and increased support from within University of Washington administration.

Analysis

After careful analysis, the primary takeaways of what Washington eSports could do to improve eSports at UW were the following:

  • Allow students to tryout out on an individual basis and ensure the tryout proccess is transparent and fair to everyone.
  • Maintain strong relationships with the communities that support Washington eSports teams while still allowing them to focus on winning.
  • Create a standardized structure to approach practicing and competiting.
  • Deploy consistent oversight to assist teams in meeting their goals and to resolve conflicts.
  • Create a brand that represents UW across all games in a way that is consistent, professional, and promotes UW values.
  • Allow players to solely focus on playing the game to the best of their abilities.
  • Promote a unified and organized lobbying effort to connect with the UW administration for recognition and increased support.

Brand Creation

Washington eSports' branding is primarily based off of University of Washington’s branding. By emulating UW’s brand and tweaking it we demonstrate our association with UW while still being able to reach out to an audience that likes to be on the cutting edge.

Examples

Organizational Documentation

In order to ensure that the expectations of players, coaches, and managers are met, I created a player manual for people to reference that overviewed team policies, resources at their disposal, along with an outline of what is expected from each position. This manual was one of the first to be created for a collegiate eSports organization and was featured at Riot Games Collegiate Summit.

Along with the player manual, I also drafted the alpha version of Washington eSports constitution and operation guide. While drafting the constitution I designed it to be light and flexible due to the rapid pace at which the collegiate eSports scene is changing and at which Washington eSports will change in order to find its place within the community at UW.
